My Story

I got my first "Rubik's Cube" in 2017 with me trying to make patterns and trying to solve a side. I would occasionally challenge myself at the time and try to do a pattern on one side (such as a green "L" [Luigi], checkerboard patterns, flags, etc.). This is about where I got to before it living on my desk for about a year, being a decoration piece on its stand with me occasionally solving a side or two, but it remaining mostly unsolved. 2018 is when cubing really started for me, with the GATE test being finished, I had nothing to do, so my mother made me, instead of playing video games, to try and solve the Rubik's Cube that sat on my shelf. I had no motivation at the time, with me just messing around with the cube and being happy if I solved a face. My mother gave my brother the same challenge and bought him a Rubik's Cube as well. I sort of forgot about the challenge over the next week, but I seemed to gravitate to my Rubik's Cube and mess arouns with it more than I used to before. After my brother learnt how to solve the Rubik's Cube, it gave me the incentive to start learning, with me being lazy at first, using a Rubik's Cube solver and seeing what it does. It was after a few solves that I realized that it has no pattern that I could learn, so I faced my fears and went on the internet and looked for a tutorial. After hours of trying, I found a website called "You can do the Cube" and it had some helpful things, such as how to turn the cube and not just notation and some simple algorithms. I got to work and started to learn the algorithms with me learning how to solve the first layer in a breeze, the second layer in about an hour. After this came the most tedious part, learning how to solve the last layer.
















This was challenging (and sort of still is to this day, although I use a different method), as I had to learn quite a few algoritms. I pushed through, and I learnt the necessary algorithms, and was able to solve the cube in about 5 minutes. At this point, my brother could solve it in about 1 minute. This is where I started to get competi tive with my brother, so my times dropped by 4 minutes in about 1 week, so I became competitive with my brother and we would race a lot, but he would just about always win. So, I decided to take this more seriously and try and beat him. After 2 weeks of practicing in all of my free time , I learnt 2 new methods, CFOP and Roux (I use both methods today as well) and I soon averaged under the (magical for me) 1 minute mark, so I was able to beat my brother. After this, I kept going as I had the incentive to get faster and faster, so soon came under 50 seconds, then under 40 seconds, then 30 seconds, and that is where I am today, with me very close to consistantly averaging under 20 seconds, with me globally averaging 21.47 seconds on 3x3. I now do more events, with me being the best at (in the context of the rest of the world) Square-1.
